        <div class="inner">
            <header class="major">
                <h2>About AMod</h2>
            </header>

            <p>
                AMod was never meant for publishing, and never was published. It was only a small learning project with 
                a tiny timeframe of 1 weekend to learn all the steps involved in Source Engine modding. <br><br>
                
                I set myself a goal of adding three new weapons to Half-Life 2, having to learn everything from importing files,
                authoring new child-classes, and ensuring compatability with Source tools, such as the Map Editor, Hammer. <br><br>

                After just 3 days, I had a functioning set of new weapons, and had become familiar enough with the engine
                to apply to the Vance team, to help contribute with that project!
            </p>
            
            <p>
                Important skills I learned or improved:
            </p>
            
             <div class="table-wrapper">
                <table>
                    <thead>
                        <tr>
                            <th>Skill</th>
                            <th>Description</th>
                        </tr>

                    <tbody>
                        <tr>
                            <td>Setting achievable goals and challenges</td>
                            <td>Using new tech can be hard, but not trying enough things with it may let you to not try at all.
                            So I set myself a goal of 3 new weapons with unique mechanics, to be well familiar with many things.
                            The Grenade launcher would be working with projectiles, and a unique firing system, the G11 taught
                            basic loops and adding new ammo types and icons, and the wrench was what taught me how damage and
                            logic loops, and assets / animations work in Source.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>Delving into new tools</td>
                            <td>The Source Engine is huge and very segmented. Each part of the engine is in it's own executable
                            and the C++ code acts as the meeting point. Having to use all of these, and get familiar with them
                            so fast, proved to myself I can learn new things easily if I need to.</td>
                        </tr>
                        <tr>
                            <td>Tailoring my skills</td>
                            <td>While I was already quite familiar with Unreal and their C++ code base, learning a new one
                            and one which is 20 years old, challenged me to drop my standards and assumptions from other frameworks.
                            It helped me break out of just knowing C++ for Unreal, and was a great benefit when I applied 
                            to be a programmer for Vance later that month.</td>
                        </tr>
