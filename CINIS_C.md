            <header class="major">
                <h2>About CINIS - Classic</h2>
            </header>

            <p>
                The original CINIS started as a school project, with me and a fellow student working on it. The original
                idea was a "Rogue-like QUAKE". Initial prototypes dabbled in procedurally generated levels, different types
                of objectives per level, and different progression mechanics. <br><br>

                Eventually it ended on the last version of the game, Alpha 1.8, where development ended due to overwhelming
                technical debt, and a lack of a clear creative vision going forward. <br><br>
                
                But, despite all of that, I learned a lot about game dev, as it was my first publically released game,
                with continous content updates. Looking back, a lot of things were done either the wrong way, or the hard way,
                but it reminds me of how much I learned!
            </p>
